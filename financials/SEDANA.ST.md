## Product

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Product | AnaConDa / Sedaconda ACD — passive reflector device enabling volatile anaesthetic (isoflurane/sevoflurane) administration in ICU |
| Mechanism | Device attached to ventilator breathing circuit; ~90%+ exhaled volatile gas reflector saves cost + limits scavenging requirements |
| EU status | CE-marked; NICE-recommended (UK); dominant EU installed base (~2.3M devices) |
| US status | FDA NDA filed **June 2026** (drug NDA for isoflurane/Sedaconda); Fast Track designation; decision expected **Q1–Q2 2027** |
| FDA program type | **Drug NDA** (not device 510(k)/PMA) — isoflurane is the pharmaceutical; AnaConDa is the delivery device |
| Pivotal trial | INSPiRE-ICU (SED003–004) — broad adult ICU sedation vs. propofol; non-inferiority met |
| US label risk | Broad adult ICU sedation is the target; narrowed "difficult-to-sedate" label would shrink TAM |

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Device | Status | Assessment |
|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| MIRUS (TIM GmbH, Germany) | Active (electronic) volatile delivery; desflurane + isoflurane + sevoflurane | CE-marked; commercially available EU | Real competitor; technically superior in some aspects (automated dosing, monitoring); BUT real-world data shows only ~14% usage / ~10% awareness vs 84%/91% for Sedaconda (French VOL'ICU survey). NOT a major market share threat. |
| AnaConDa | Passive reflector | EU dominant (84% real-world share) | Installed-base, clinical data, NICE, simpler/cheaper design |

> ⚠ MOAT CLAIM CORRECTED: AnaConDa is **NOT** the sole EU ICU volatile anaesthesia device. MIRUS by TIM GmbH is a direct CE-marked competitor. The correct moat claim is "dominant installed-base leader with 84% real-world market share, NICE endorsement, and 10+ years of clinical data." The moat is real; the "sole" claim was wrong.
