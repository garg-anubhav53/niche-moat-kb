## Business Model

**Diaceutics** operates the Precision Medicine Implementation (PMI) platform — a data analytics and execution platform that connects pharmaceutical companies (sponsors) to diagnostic laboratory networks for companion diagnostic (CDx) testing programs.

**How it works:** When a pharma company launches a targeted therapy (e.g., an oncology drug requiring a specific biomarker test), Diaceutics connects the pharma sponsor to the fragmented lab network that actually runs the diagnostic tests. Diaceutics has agreements with 2,500+ labs globally and sits between pharma sponsors and the lab ecosystem as a data and execution intermediary.

**Revenue model:** Mostly SaaS/platform fees from pharma companies paying for CDx program execution services and data access. High gross margin (87%) is consistent with this software/data model.

## Moat Assessment (Preliminary — §4)

**Moat type claim:** NETWORK EFFECT + DATA ASSET (lab connectivity network with 2,500+ agreements; proprietary CDx testing data across cancer types; switching cost for pharma sponsors = re-qualifying a new vendor and re-onboarding 2,500+ labs)

**Moat strength:** Moderate-high. Network effects are real — the more labs and pharma sponsors on the platform, the more valuable the data; but this is not a regulatory monopoly (pharma could theoretically build their own lab network or use a competitor). Key competitive advantage is the existing 2,500+ lab agreement base and accumulated historical CDx testing data.

**Key unknowns (unverified at §4):**
1. How many competing platforms exist? (ConcertAI, Veracyte's lab network, Guardant Health data) — DXRX.L's uniqueness vs. these not confirmed
2. Customer concentration — what % of revenue from top 3 pharma sponsors?
3. Revenue visibility — what % is recurring subscription vs. project-based?
