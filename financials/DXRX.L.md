## Business Model

**Diaceutics** operates the Precision Medicine Implementation (PMI) platform — a data analytics and execution platform that connects pharmaceutical companies (sponsors) to diagnostic laboratory networks for companion diagnostic (CDx) testing programs.

**How it works:** When a pharma company launches a targeted therapy (e.g., an oncology drug requiring a specific biomarker test), Diaceutics connects the pharma sponsor to the fragmented lab network that actually runs the diagnostic tests. Diaceutics has agreements with 2,500+ labs globally and sits between pharma sponsors and the lab ecosystem as a data and execution intermediary.

**Revenue model:** ~52% recurring (ARR platform fees) + ~48% project-based (CDx program execution). Gross margin 82% (FY2025). Revenue quality is mixed: ARR NRR of 105% is weak for a SaaS/data business; nearly half of revenue is lumpy project work.

**PMx concentration risk (§5 OPUS confirmed):** ~21% of ARR from 2 contracts tied to third-party drug success. These are contingent on pharma sponsor programmes not failing in clinical trials.

## Moat Assessment (§4 preliminary + §5 OPUS updated)

**Moat type claim:** NETWORK EFFECT + DATA ASSET (lab connectivity network with 2,500+ agreements; proprietary CDx testing data across cancer types; switching cost for pharma sponsors = re-qualifying a new vendor and re-onboarding 2,500+ labs)

**Moat strength (OPUS 3yr/5yr/10yr durability):**
- **3yr:** Moderate. Network real; 2,500+ lab agreements create real switching friction for pharma; data asset accumulating. Competitors nascent.
- **5yr:** Weakening. ConcertAI + Foundation Medicine (Roche) both building pharma-facing data platforms; CDx market consolidation possible; NRR 105% suggests limited pricing power expansion.
- **10yr:** Uncertain. If ConcertAI or a Roche/AZ in-house solution becomes standard, Diaceutics faces disintermediation. No regulatory exclusivity to protect position.

**Key risks confirmed at §5:**
1. Revenue quality: only 52% ARR; NRR 105% weak for a platform claiming network effects
2. Customer concentration: ~21% ARR from 2 PMx contracts tied to third-party drug outcomes
3. Cash conversion: £7.6M adj EBITDA → -£5.3M actual cash outflow; FCF negative
4. Competitive set: ConcertAI, Foundation Medicine, Guardant Health all building adjacent capabilities
5. GM step-down FY2024→FY2025 (88%→82%) is structural (incremental data costs) not one-time
