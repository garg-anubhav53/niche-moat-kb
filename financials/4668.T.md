## §4 Verdict: PARK 5/12

**Reason for PARK:**
- **No durable moat identified:** Cram school (juku) market in Japan is highly competitive and fragmented. Major competitors include Kumon, SAPIX, Eikoh, Sundai, Ichishin-kai, and hundreds of independent operators. Meiko operates a franchise model (some asset-light characteristics) but franchise moat is minimal — switching to a competing franchised or independent juku is low-cost and common for Japanese families.
- **Structural headwind:** Japan's school-age population (ages 6-18) is in secular decline due to birth rate trends. Total public primary school enrollment has been falling every year. This is a durable demand headwind, not a cyclical one.
- **Anti-value-trap check:** P/E 10.24x appears cheap but reflects the structural decline expectations. Quality is insufficient to justify a deep-dive.
- **Quality=1 assessment:** Net income ~¥1.72B on revenue that needs primary filing confirmation. Even if profitable, the business lacks the pricing power or regulatory moat that would justify a deeper look. Per ROUTINE.md anti-value-trap rule: Quality ≤ 1 → PARK, do not deep-dive.
