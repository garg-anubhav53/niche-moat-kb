# Niche Moat KB — Cross-Industry Asymmetric Upside

## Doctrine: Moat-First Re-Rating

**The edge:** Find companies where the moat is so defensible and so undervalued that when the market eventually recognizes it (2–5 year horizon), the stock can 2–3x on multiple expansion alone, with minimal downside risk because the moat itself IS the floor.

**Avoid:** Defense, commodities, commoditizing markets, companies with deteriorating unit economics.

**Screen criteria (all required):**
- Market cap: $20M–$300M (sweet spot for institutional exclusion + not too small to fail)
- Daily dollar volume: <$3M average (thick enough to enter, thin enough for asymmetry)
- Sell-side analysts: 0–3 (coverage void = re-rating catalyst when analyst initiates)
- Moat type: sole-source, qualification lock-in, regulatory certification, proprietary process, high switching cost, or structural scarcity
- Revenue: recurring/consumable/contract-backed (not lumpy one-time sales)
- Gross margin: >40% (indicates pricing power from moat, not commodity)
- Net debt: <1.0x EBITDA or net cash (floor is protected)

**Exclusion criteria (any one kills):**
- 12-month return >+100% (asymmetry already spent)
- Negative book equity (floor is fake)
- Active bankruptcy or delisting notice
- >4 sell-side analysts (coverage void gone)
- Unresolved accounting issues

**Re-rating catalysts to watch:**
- First analyst initiation (10–20% pop)
- Strategic contract win with named customer
- Regulatory certification or approval
- Capacity constraint becomes binding (shortage = pricing power)
- M&A as acquiree (strategic buyer recognizes moat value)

## Accumulated Macro Observations

1. **Regulatory certification is the stickiest moat.** FDA, FAA, NRC, ISO approvals create multi-year requalification friction that survives competitive pressure.
2. **Niche equipment makers with >40% gross margins are almost always moat-driven.** Commodity makers run 20–30%. The margin gap is the moat signal.
3. **Consumables with installed-base stickiness are re-rating machines.** Once an equipment OEM specifies your consumable in their tool, the customer locks in — and the installed base replaces annually.
4. **Small-cap acquisition targets.** Strategic acquirers value moats at 3–6x revenues (vs. market pricing at 1–2x). Identifying names that are acquisition-attractive is itself a re-rating catalyst.
5. **Foreign listings (Japan, Germany, UK mid-cap) with US OTC exposure are information deserts.** English-language coverage is sparse; analyst initiation re-rates the stock 20–40%.
