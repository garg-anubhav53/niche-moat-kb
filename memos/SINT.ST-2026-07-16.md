## Business Summary

SinterCast AB (Nasdaq Stockholm: SINT.ST; US OTC: SINTF) is a Swedish pure-IP company and the **only commercial provider of real-time Compacted Graphite Iron (CGI) process control technology** globally. CGI is a specialty cast iron microstructure that combines the thermal and mechanical performance of grey iron with improved strength — used primarily in diesel/heavy-duty engine blocks (cylinder blocks, heads, exhaust manifolds) where the combination outperforms both grey iron and aluminium at high thermal loads.

SinterCast's patented thermal-analysis system monitors molten iron microstructure in real time at the foundry ladle. Foundries need this control because CGI is extremely sensitive to magnesium content (±0.001% determines whether you get CGI, grey iron, or nodular iron). There is **no substitute** for this real-time measurement in commercial CGI production. SinterCast licenses the system to foundries under a royalty model (~$0.95/kg of CGI produced + equipment purchase/lease) and currently has ~400 production units installed at OEM foundries worldwide.

**Primary customers (indirect — foundries that supply):** Ford (diesel truck blocks: F-150 Power Stroke, Super Duty, Transit), Volvo (commercial vehicle engines), Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Scania, and other heavy-duty OEMs. All CGI engine blocks in commercial production globally use SinterCast technology.

**Key financials (confirmed Q1 2026 / TTM):**
- Revenue: SEK 25.0M Q1 2026 (−7.7% YoY; partly −14.4% USD/SEK FX); TTM ~SEK 117M implied from net margin
- Net income TTM: ~SEK 28.1M
- Net margin: 23.92%
- ROE: 33% · ROA: 21%
- Market cap: SEK 753.5M (~$73M USD) at ~SEK 95/share
- Analyst consensus PT: ~SEK 95 (stock AT or above PT — confirming absence of asymmetric entry today)
- Analyst count: 2–3 Swedish analysts; zero US coverage

**Revenue trajectory:** −20% FY2025 YoY driven by structural EV-transition headwind (fewer CGI-alloy ICE engine blocks ordered by OEMs pivoting to EV powertrain programs) and USD/SEK FX headwind (~14% of decline). Q1 2026 revenue continues soft at −7.7% YoY. The EV transition directly reduces CGI engine block volume in the passenger vehicle segment; heavy truck, off-road, and industrial CGI applications are growing but not large enough to offset passenger vehicle attrition.

## §5 Deep-Dive — Four Sub-Assessments

### Q — Business Quality: 3/5

**What it gets right:**
- Textbook capital-light royalty model: 23.92% net margin, 33% ROE, 21% ROA — exceptional return metrics for an industrial company
- Genuinely the only commercial CGI process control provider globally for 25+ years; no competitor has replicated the thermal analysis system at foundry scale
- Royalty per-kilogram CGI produced = recurring revenue tied to volumes, not capital projects
- Owner-operator culture; long-tenured management team; conservative guidance delivery

**What limits it to 3 (not 4 or 5):**
- Revenue is in structural decline: −20% FY2025 and continuing soft in Q1 2026; the TAM (CGI engine blocks in ICE passenger vehicles) is shrinking as EV adoption accelerates
- Reinvestment runway is narrowing: where do you redeploy capital when your core application is shrinking?
- Revenue durability depends on CGI engine block volumes at Ford/Volvo/Mercedes foundries, which are driven by decisions made by OEMs above the foundry level — SinterCast has no influence over OEM product roadmaps
- Q=3: solid, defensible moat + exceptional returns, but declining TAM prevents a "franchise compounder" (Q=4-5) rating; this is a harvesting business, not a reinvesting one

### F — Downside Floor: 4/5

**Floor mechanics:**
- Profitable at current reduced volumes (TTM net income ~SEK 28.1M even with −20% revenue in FY2025)
- Capital-light: near-zero physical assets; no inventory risk; no capital equipment depreciation drag
- No disclosed debt; no going-concern; no dilution history
- The royalty model means fixed costs are extremely low; SinterCast can be profitable at much lower revenue than today
- Even at SEK 60-70M revenue (a further −40% from current levels), the company would likely remain profitable given ~23-25% net margin at current scale
- No debt signals; positive equity confirmed; dividend-paying history

**What prevents F=5:**
- The structural revenue decline creates a "permanently impaired" risk: if CGI engine block volumes collapse faster than expected (e.g., EU banning ICE heavy trucks by 2035), the royalty stream could halve in a single product-cycle refresh
- Cash balance not confirmed precisely; approximate net cash probably SEK 40-60M but not explicitly verified

**Floor rating: 4/5** — earns strongly through trough, capital-light, no leverage, unlikely to destroy capital permanently; but not "hard to lose money permanently" because the royalty stream is exposed to secular volume decline rather than a true recession-proof demand base.

### R — Re-Rate Likelihood & Proximity: 1/5

**Why R is very low:**
1. **No catalyst inside 12 months.** No hard-dated event forces a re-rating: no analyst initiations (Swedish coverage already present; US investors have little incentive to initiate on a declining-revenue SEK 750M cap company in a niche that is out of favor), no major contract wins (CGI volumes are declining, not growing), no product launches, no regulatory milestones.

3. **Secular headwind is visible and consensus.** The EV transition reducing ICE engine block production is universally understood by the 2-3 Swedish analysts who follow SINT.ST. There is no "hidden" misunderstanding to exploit.

4. **Potential new applications are slow.** CGI for wind turbine components (main shafts, bearing housings), industrial compressors, and marine engines exists as a theoretical growth area, but: (a) volumes are small relative to automotive, (b) commercialization is multi-year, (c) the royalty per-kg rate is the same regardless of application, so new application wins would need massive volume to offset automotive decline.

5. **Historical base rate suggests low re-rate probability** (see below).

**R=1/5**: no visible path to a near-term re-rating; the business is priced at consensus fair value with the secular decline risk acknowledged.

## Historical Base Rate — Analogous Companies

**Pattern sought:** Single-application measurement/process-control monopolies facing secular demand decline in their primary market.

1. **LiqTech International (LIQT):** Silicon carbide ceramic membrane filters; niche sole-source in diesel aftertreatment + marine scrubbers; re-rated +3-4x in 2019-2021 on IMO 2020 marine scrubber adoption surge, then declined as penetration plateaued and the underlying diesel exhaust market stagnated. **Did NOT achieve durable public-market re-rate** — the initial re-rate was cyclical demand, not a structural re-rating of the business quality. Post-spike, the stock settled ~1x revenue.

2. **X-Rite Incorporated (XRIT — acquired by Danaher):** Color measurement and management software monopoly (spectrophotometers, colorimetry software); genuinely the only professional-grade color measurement system globally; traded in the public market at 2-3x revenue for years, then acquired by Danaher at ~3-4x revenue. **Re-rating came through acquisition not public-market discovery.** Swing factor: strategic acquirer (Danaher) recognized the monopoly value before equity market fully priced it.

3. **Analogic Corporation (ALOG — acquired by Altaris):** Medical imaging subsystem monopoly (ultrasound + CT detector subsystems); deep moat but niche; remained range-bound in public markets for years, then taken private at 2-3x revenue. **No public-market re-rate; acquired.** Same pattern as X-Rite.

4. **Periphonics/Ultrasonics (various small-cap process control niche names):** Multiple examples of process control monopolies in niche industrial applications (ultrasonic testing, specialized flow measurement, specialty spectroscopy) — consistent pattern: they earn well (20-30% net margins), compound slowly, and eventually get acquired by a measurement conglomerate (Ametek, Danaher, Fortive, Spectris) at 3-5x revenue.

**Base rate: 1/4 re-rated significantly in public markets before acquisition; 3/4 were either acquired or remained range-bound at modest premiums.** Swing factor: whether a strategic acquirer (Ametek, Danaher, Fortive, Hexagon, Spectris) assigns control-premium valuation to the sole-source measurement monopoly, OR whether new non-automotive CGI applications generate visible growth that equity market can underwrite.
