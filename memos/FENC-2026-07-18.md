## Product & Regulatory Context

**PEDMARK® (STS):**
- Mechanism: Sodium thiosulfate chelates cisplatin metabolites in the cochlea, preventing ototoxic damage
- Indication: Prevents cisplatin-related hearing loss in pediatric cancer patients; given IV after each cisplatin dose
- Pivotal data: SIOPEL 6 randomized controlled trial (Canadian Cancer Society); enrolled ~EUZ+ international sites; statistically significant reduction in ototoxicity
- FDA approval: August 17, 2022 — Priority Review + Breakthrough Therapy Designation + Orphan Drug Designation
- **Orphan drug exclusivity:** 7 years from FDA approval = through **August 2029** (statutory; generic STS cannot receive FDA approval for this indication during this period)
- **Pediatric data exclusivity:** Additional 6 months = February 2030
- **Orange Book patents:** Method-of-use + formulation patents listed; exact expiry dates unverified (must check Orange Book or 10-K patent table)
- EMA status: Conditional marketing authorization application filed; no approval date announced as of Q1 2026

**Revenue ramp:** $1.54M (FY2022 partial) → $21.3M (FY2023) → $29.6M (FY2024) → $44.6M (FY2025) → $15.1M Q1 2026. At $15.1M quarterly, the business is annualizing >$60M and first achieved profitability in Q1 2026.

## §5 Sub-Assessments

### Q — Business Quality: **3/5**

**Returns on capital:** High once at scale. 91.6% gross margin is pharmaceutical pricing power. But SG&A (sales force, market access, medical affairs) is large relative to revenue — management is investing heavily in commercialization. Only now approaching sustained profitability.

**Margin level & direction:** Gross margins strong (91.6%). Net margin -22.6% FY2025, but inflecting: Q1 2026 net income +$0.2M. The operating leverage trajectory is clear — each incremental dollar of revenue flows through at high gross margin, and fixed costs are largely set.

**Reinvestment runway:** Constrained. PEDMARK serves ~4,000-6,000 pediatric cisplatin patients annually in the US. At current revenue ($44.6M FY2025) and estimated ~$16-18K average net price per course, penetration is already ~55-70% of eligible US patients. The remaining US penetration runway is limited. Ex-US (EU, Japan, other markets pending regulatory approval) is the primary growth vector, but EMA approval timing is unknown.

**Revenue durability & recurrence:** Durable through ~2029-2030. Every cohort of pediatric cisplatin patients is a new set of "customers" (pediatric oncologists). Revenue is recurring in the sense that new patients continuously emerge (cancer doesn't go away), not in the SaaS sense of annual contracts. Revenue drops only if cisplatin-based regimens fall out of use or if FDA/EMA restricts PEDMARK — neither is likely through 2029.

**Moat DURABILITY:** Strong through 2029, then challenged.
- Statutory FDA orphan exclusivity: no generic STS can receive FDA approval for this indication through August 2029. This is unambiguous.
- Beyond 2029: off-label generic IV sodium thiosulfate (extremely cheap; widely available in hospitals as an antidote and for cyanide poisoning) could be used by oncologists. PEDMARK's moat after exclusivity expiry depends entirely on (a) method-of-use patent enforcement and (b) label expansion to new indications that reset the exclusivity clock.
- Clinical data moat is secondary but real: PEDMARK's SIOPEL 6 RCT data took ~20 years of research. A competitor would need to run a new pediatric oncology RCT — ethically complex, expensive, slow. This provides de facto protection even after exclusivity expiry.

**Management & capital allocation:** Track record looks good operationally (50% YoY revenue growth, debt eliminated, $42M equity offering oversubscribed). But the $10.1M net loss in FY2025 despite 50% revenue growth suggests high spending growth. The equity offering was dilutive — but oversubscribed, suggesting strong institutional interest. Insider alignment not verified (must check proxy for % ownership).

**Score rationale:** Q=3 (solid, defensible through 2029) rather than 4 because: single product, visible patent cliff in ~3 years, limited reinvestment runway in current indication, early profitability (only Q1 2026). A franchise compounder would have a pipeline or multi-product platform.

## Historical Base Rate & Analog Process

**Pattern:** Single-product orphan drug company, sole FDA-approved agent for pediatric/rare indication, approaching profitability during commercial ramp, 0-1 analyst coverage at $150-350M market cap.

**Analogs:**

1. **Sucampo Pharmaceuticals (SCMP)** — sole FDA-approved secretagogue for opioid-induced constipation (amitiza, lubiprostone). Cap ~$200-400M during ramp phase. Re-rated 2-3x as revenue scaled and profitability confirmed, then acquired by Mallinckrodt at ~$1.2B (5x+ from trough). **Outcome: 2x+ in ~3 years; swing factor = sustained profitability + analyst discovery.**

2. **Retrophin / Thiola (tiopronin, cystinuria)** — sole FDA-approved treatment for cystinuria. Revenue ramp similar to FENC. Orphan exclusivity. Re-rated 3-4x over 2-3 years before management/governance issues arose. **Outcome: 3-4x; swing factor = penetration ceiling and management credibility.**

3. **Horizon Therapeutics early phase (HRZNP, FY2013-2016)** — single-product orphan phase (KRYSTEXXA for gout); ~$50-100M revenue; approaching profitability; 0-2 analysts. Re-rated 5-8x over 3-4 years as profitability confirmed and pipeline added. **Outcome: 5-8x; swing factor = profitability confirmation + additional products (pipeline).**

4. **Enzon Pharmaceuticals** — earlier analog; single cancer-supportive-care product; re-rated on profitability then mean-reverted on patent cliff + no pipeline. **Outcome: partial re-rate then decline; swing factor = no pipeline to offset patent cliff.**

**Base rate:** "Companies like this re-rated 3 of 4 times (75%); the swing factor was whether (a) profitability confirmed by 2+ consecutive quarters AND (b) second indication or ex-US market opened within 3 years of initial approval. The 1/4 failure occurred when neither (a) nor (b) materialized before the patent cliff was visible."

**FENC positioning vs base rate:** Q1 2026 first profit ✓ (beginning of (a)). EMA approval could supply (b) if it comes within 2027. Adult/carboplatin label expansion is a longer-dated (b). Base rate is supportive but FENC needs at least one more quarter of profitability and a visible ex-US path to confirm the analog.
