## The Five Asymmetry Questions

### 1. Is the moat real and durable?

**CONFIRMED — strong PROCESS + SOLE_SOURCE moat.**

Kromek grows cadmium zinc telluride (CZT) crystals at commercial scale — one of fewer than 4-5 global producers capable of detector-grade CZT. CZT is a room-temperature semiconductor that offers significantly superior gamma-ray energy resolution vs. traditional NaI scintillators: tighter photopeak = more precise isotope identification (critical for SPECT cardiac imaging, nuclear security, CBRN detection).

**Named OEM lock-in — Siemens Healthineers:** KMK disclosed $11.7M Enablement Agreement revenue in FY26 from Siemens, plus ongoing OEM CZT component supply for Siemens cardiac SPECT detectors. Siemens' SPECT camera geometry and detector electronics are engineered around KMK's CZT specification. Switching would require: (a) requalifying an alternate CZT source (3-5 year timeline), (b) re-engineering detector geometry, (c) FDA/CE re-submission. This is textbook sole-source switching cost.

**Competitive position:** Main global CZT producers are Kromek (UK), Redlen Technologies (private, Canada), and H3D (private, US). No public competitor at scale. Chinese producers have CZT quality challenges for medical-grade use. After 15+ years of continuous manufacturing, KMK has a process and yield advantage that is difficult to replicate.

**Who's tried and failed:** Numerous academic and startup efforts at CZT manufacturing have struggled with boule growth reproducibility and detector yield. KMK's in-house crystal growing and detector production is deeply integrated.

### 2. What is the floor?

**CONDITIONAL earnings floor — not a fortress.**

FY26 financials: revenue £27.2M, PBT £2.15M (7.9% margin). Positive equity, no major debt flags. The company is profitable.

**Key risk: Siemens concentration.** The Siemens Enablement Agreement = ~$11.7M (£9.3M) = 34% of FY26 total revenue. If the Enablement Agreement expires without renewal, revenue drops to ~£18M. At £18M revenue with current fixed manufacturing costs (CZT crystal growth facility has significant fixed overhead), profitability is uncertain — could approach breakeven or thin losses.

**Mitigation:** KMK's CBRN Detection segment is growing independently. H2 FY26 new orders = £8.8M. If CBRN continues to grow, partial offset. But the Siemens agreement is the key swing factor.

**Floor verdict:** Positive floor if Siemens relationship continues (profitable, growing, covered fixed costs). Conditional floor if Siemens expires — not a durable "earns on zero new orders" floor. SCORE: Floor = 1 (not the durable earnings floor needed for WATCH).

### 5. What kills it?

**Single thesis-killer:** Siemens Healthineers terminates or does not renew the Enablement Agreement (~CY2026 or CY2027 decision point). Revenue collapses from £27M to ~£18M. At £18M revenue, KMK approaches breakeven. Multiple compresses to 1-1.5x P/S = £18-27M cap (-53 to -69% from today). Stock likely returns to 4-5p range.

**Secondary risks:**
