## Business Overview

Eckert & Ziegler SE is Germany's leading producer of radioactive components for medicine and industry. Founded in 1997 by Dr. Andreas Eckert (physicist / serial nuclear-tech entrepreneur), the company operates across three segments:

**Medical (55% rev, 64% EBIT adj):** Contract manufacturing of radiopharmaceutical precursors — supplies n.c.a. Lutetium-177 chloride (Theralugand®) to CDMO chains serving Novartis Pluvicto (Lu-177-PSMA-617); Gallium-68 (GalliaPharm® = FDA/EMA-approved Ga-68 generator, market leader); Actinium-225 supply agreements (Actinium Pharmaceuticals, GlyTherix, SK Biopharmaceuticals, Bicycle Therapeutics, Molecular Partners); Yttrium-90 and Copper-64/67 targets; Iodine-125 brachytherapy seeds. Revenue +16% YoY on radiopharma demand.

**Isotope Products (45% rev, 36% EBIT adj):** Global market leader in radioactive calibration and reference sources (Isotrak™ product line) — every radiation instrument in hospitals/nuclear plants/research labs requires annual recalibration against a known source. EUZ manufactures Cs-137, Co-60, Am-241, Sr-90, Na-22, and 100+ other calibration standards from Valencia CA, Atlanta GA, and Braunschweig Germany facilities. Also: oil well logging sources (Cs-137, Am-241-Be neutron sources for oil/gas formation evaluation) and blood irradiators (Cs-137 for bone marrow transplant preparation = regulated, inelastic demand). Industrial analytics standards for environmental monitoring, nuclear plant QA.

**2025 license income:** €15M (presumably IP licensing from existing technology agreements); declining to €5M in 2026 — creates a €10M headwind on reported earnings. Organic growth excluding this: revenue +9%, EBIT +21%.

## §5 Sub-Assessments (1–5 scale)

### Q — Business Quality: 4/5

**Evidence:**
- ROIC 15.03%, ROE 17.72% — earns well above cost of capital in a specialty regulated manufacturing niche
- Net margin 15.6% FY2025 (€48.8M / €312M), expanding — net income +47% on +5% revenue = operating leverage
- Revenue structure: calibration sources = annual replacement cycle (hospital instrument recalibration mandated by NRC/BfS/IAEA), structurally recurring. CDMO supply agreements = multi-year contracts with big pharma (Novartis, Actinium)
- Moat durability: BfS (German federal radiation authority) licensing + NRC (US) licensing for radioactive source manufacturing. New entrant must: (a) obtain a broad materials license from the NRC (typically 2+ years), (b) build shielded manufacturing facilities (capital-intensive), (c) demonstrate quality systems for radioactive material handling, (d) qualify product with every end-customer laboratory (18-24 months per customer). This is a textbook barrier-to-entry franchise.
- Growing TAM: theranostics (targeted radionuclide therapy) is one of oncology's fastest-growing segments. Pluvicto alone targeting ~$3B revenue by 2028 (Novartis guidance); every vial requires Lu-177 CDMO supply. Ac-225 is next wave (Alpha-therapy pipelines from Novartis, AstraZeneca, Eli Lilly progressing Phase III trials)
- Capital allocation: net cash (€63.8M); dividend increased at 2026 AGM; no serial dilution; founder-family culture with long-term orientation
- Management credibility: guidance delivery track record strong; 2025 = "another record year"; Q1 2026 beat consensus on radioisotope demand

**Capping factor from 5:** Near-term license income roll-off (€10M headwind in 2026) creates earnings optics headwind; Lu-177 is NOT a monopoly position (competitors exist — ITG, Curium, GE Healthcare); management succession beyond Dr. Eckert unclear.
