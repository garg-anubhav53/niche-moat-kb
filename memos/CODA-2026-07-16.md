# CODA — Coda Octopus Group — Deep-Dive Memo

## Business in one paragraph
Coda Octopus Group (NASDAQ: CODA) makes the Echoscope® — the world's only commercially proven real-time 3D sonar capable of imaging *moving* objects underwater in zero-visibility conditions. Deployed on survey vessels and remotely operated platforms, it serves offshore energy (oil, gas, offshore wind), port construction and security, hydrographic survey, and maritime defense customers. Revenue FY2025: $26.6M (+30.7% YoY), gross margin 66.5%, net cash ~$30.4M, market cap ~$140M. Transitioning from lumpy hardware sales to higher-margin recurring software + support contracts.

---

## Five Asymmetry Questions

### 1. Is the moat real and durable?

**YES — patent-protected sole commercial product.**

The Echoscope® patent covers real-time volumetric sonar (3D imaging of moving objects). No competitor has a commercially deployed equivalent:
- Kongsberg (Norway), Tritech (UK), and Teledyne have conventional 2D multibeam sonar — useful for static seafloor mapping, but useless for visualizing a diver, a moving cable, or a swinging construction tool in real-time 3D.
- The physics of Echoscope's signal processing — producing real-time 3D imagery at 12+ frames/sec underwater — is a software-hardware combination that took 15+ years to develop and productize. Competitors have not replicated it in the decade CODA has been commercializing the technology.

**Switching cost:** Customers that integrate Echoscope commit to retraining sonar operators, modifying vessel data workflows, and using CODA's NavPac integrated navigation suite. Requalification to a hypothetical alternative (if one existed) would take 12–24 months and require operational downtime on expensive survey vessels.

**Who's tried:** No publicly documented competing product exists. CODA's 15-year commercial track record — with growing revenue and zero credible displacement — is the best evidence moat is real.

**Durability caveat:** This is a small (~150-person) company. Key-person concentration in R&D leadership is a risk. The patent lifespan and a hypothetical Kongsberg/Teledyne 5-year R&D sprint are legitimate tail risks.

**Moat score: 2/2**

**No serial dilution visible.** No going-concern. No covenant risk.

**Floor score: 2/2**

### 3. What is the specific 2x re-rating catalyst and timing?

**This is where the WATCH case fails.**

Three candidate catalysts are visible, none hard-dated:

**(a) US Navy DAVD Untethered fleet deployment — 2H 2026 "improved order visibility"**
The DAVD (Diver Augmented Vision Display) Untethered is a diver-worn heads-up display using Echoscope imagery for port security clearance and marine construction. Freedom Broker states that the recent US Navy approval of DAVD Untethered "improves order visibility for 2H 2026, particularly for defense-related projects." However, this language describes expectation, not a signed contract. A large fleet contract ($10–25M+) would be transformative, but no announcement has been made.

**(b) Offshore wind survey framework agreement**
Offshore wind cable laying and turbine foundation inspection use Echoscope for zero-visibility conditions. A 3–5 year preferred-supplier agreement with a Tier-1 wind developer (Ørsted, Equinor, RWE) would signal recurring TAM expansion. Timing: not announced, unpredictable.

**(c) Asian market bulk adoption**
Q1 2026 called out "significant Asian market traction." Port authority adoptions in China/Taiwan/Singapore could generate bulk multi-unit orders. Timing: no specific event named.

**For a quick 2x:** stock must go from ~$140M to ~$280M cap. That requires either (1) revenue demonstrably accelerating toward $50M+ (requires continued 30%+ growth AND a large contract win), or (2) P/S multiple expanding from 5.3× to 10.5× on the same revenue (requires market re-rating of the TAM or business model). Neither is achievable without a specific large signed contract.

**Critically:** Q2 2026 revenue was −1.6% YoY, showing the business remains lumpy. The analyst's $14.00 fair-value target closely tracks the current market price. There is no asymmetric entry available at current prices.

**Catalyst score: 1/2** (vague, within 12 months, but not hard-dated <6mo binary event)

### 4. What must be true for the bull case, and is it already priced?

**Bull case requires:**
1. US Navy DAVD deployment at fleet-scale ($15–25M contract within 6–9 months)
2. Offshore wind survey adoption inflects — multiple concurrent framework contracts
3. Asian market generates a cluster of port authority orders in 12 months
4. Revenue reaches $40–50M in FY2026/FY2027 → market pays 8–10× P/S = $320–500M cap = 2.3–3.6× current

**Is it priced?**
The existing 30% FY2025 growth rate is already embedded in the $140M cap at 5.3× P/S. Q2 2026 at −1.6% YoY means the market has already partially discounted near-term lumpiness. Analyst consensus of $14.00 = exactly current market. The bull case requires an *incremental positive surprise* above current expectations — a large contract not yet in the model — not just continuation of the existing trajectory.

---

### 5. What kills it?

**(a) Revenue lumpiness delivers a flat/declining FY2026** — if offshore wind orders slip, DAVD deployments are small, and Asian market is slower than expected, FY2026 revenue could come in below FY2025's $26.6M. At 4× P/S on trough revenue = $107M cap = −24% from today.

**(b) DAVD at modest scale** — if the Navy only orders 5–10 units ($2–4M) instead of fleet-wide ($15–25M), Freedom Broker's thesis collapses and the sole covering analyst may cut PT.

**(c) Competitor replication** — Kongsberg or Teledyne dedicates an R&D team to real-time 3D sonar. Estimated timeline: 3–7 years. Tail risk, not near-term.

**(d) Key-person risk** — ~150 employees; concentrated R&D IP in a small team; departure of core engineers = IP disruption.

**(e) Offshore wind policy delays** — US IRA uncertainty, European permitting backlog → survey market slows faster than expected.
