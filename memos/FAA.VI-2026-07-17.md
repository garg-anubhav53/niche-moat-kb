## Moat Assessment

**Primary moat: REGULATORY + SWITCHING_COST (dual-layer)**

1. **Regulatory/compliance lock**: Fabasoft has held 13 of 14 Austrian federal ministry contracts since 2004. The Austrian E-Government Act mandates specific interoperability and security standards that Fabasoft's platform is certified for. Replacing an accredited ECM platform across a ministry requires re-certification, re-training, and parliamentary/administrative approval — a 2–4 year switching cycle minimum.

2. **Workflow integration depth**: Government ministries run thousands of automated workflows through Fabasoft's platform. The deeper the workflow integration, the higher the switching cost. Unlike generic SaaS, Fabasoft's integrations span archiving, signature validation, and inter-agency data routing — all tied to Austrian ELAK (electronic file management) standards.

3. **German expansion via E-Akte Bund**: Germany's federal digitalization mandate (E-Akte Bund) created a certified-vendor pathway. Fabasoft has confirmed:
   - 200+ German federal authorities on E-Akte Bund
   - Bavaria: 50,000+ users
   - Hesse: 25,000 seats
   - Rhineland-Palatinate: 17,000 users
   This is a beachhead into a market ~10× Austria's federal government in scale.

**Moat durability**: HIGH. The regulatory certification requirement creates a multi-year barrier; ECM systems are not commodity software. The Austrian position has been held for 20+ years with no churn. The German beachhead is expanding, not contracting.
