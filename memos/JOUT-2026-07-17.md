## Company Overview

Johnson Outdoors Inc. (Nasdaq: JOUT) is a multi-brand outdoor recreational products company headquartered in Racine, Wisconsin. Founded 1970 by Samuel C. Johnson; Helen Johnson-Leipold (daughter) has been CEO since 1999 — 25+ years of family stewardship. Johnson family beneficial ownership: ~50%+ combined (Class A + Class B shares). Fiscal year ends first Saturday of October (FY2025 ended October 3, 2025).

**Four business segments:**
1. **Fishing** (~80% of revenue in peak seasons): Minn Kota electric trolling motors + Cannon downriggers + Humminbird fish finders/sonar/GPS
2. **Diving** (~8-10%): SCUBAPRO premium SCUBA equipment (regulators, BCDs, wetsuits, computers), Subgear entry-level
3. **Camping & Watercraft Recreation** (~10-12%): Jetboil integrated cooking stoves; Old Town canoes/kayaks; Ocean Kayak; Carlisle paddles (mostly divested/wound down)
4. **Watercraft Recreation** (included above)

**Moat claim — BRAND + MARKET_LEADER:**
- **Minn Kota**: ~70-80% US electric trolling motor market share; market-dominant for 30+ years; proprietary i-Pilot Link GPS anchor-holding and route-following; AutoPilot feature; integrated battery management; price range $200-$3,000+
- **Humminbird**: #1 US fish finder brand; MEGA Imaging side/down/live sonar; integrated GPS charting; One-Boat Network ecosystem (Minn Kota + Humminbird + Cannon integration via proprietary i-Pilot Link Bluetooth/GPS)
- **SCUBAPRO**: Global premium SCUBA equipment brand; 60+ year history; trusted by serious divers worldwide; specifications demanded by commercial dive operators and military/rescue divers
- **Jetboil**: Specialty ultralight integrated camp stove/cooking system; strong brand in backpacking/mountaineering channel; FluxRing thermal efficiency IP

**One-Boat Network moat**: Minn Kota motors communicate with Humminbird fish finders via i-Pilot Link — a proprietary protocol that creates genuine ecosystem switching cost. An angler who purchases Minn Kota + Humminbird + Cannon (downriggers) gets integrated trolling navigation that rivals cannot easily replicate (requires redesign of both motor control firmware AND fish finder display software). Competitor combinations (Garmin Force motor + Garmin Striker finder) create a competing ecosystem, but Minn Kota's installed base of ~70-80% US trolling motors means most serious bass anglers already own Minn Kota.

## The Four Sub-Assessments

### Q — Business Quality: **3/5**

The Fishing segment is a genuine franchise: Minn Kota 70-80% US market share + Humminbird #1 are not marketing claims — they reflect 30+ years of category dominance in a market where professional bass tournament anglers demand best-in-class performance and brand matters enormously. The One-Boat Network ecosystem integration creates genuine switching cost beyond simple brand preference.

**Why not 4/5:** Consumer discretionary exposure is the ceiling here. The FY2022-2025 downturn (revenue from ~$900M to $592M) showed the business cannot escape the consumer spending cycle. The fishing market boomed in COVID-driven outdoor recreation demand, then normalized sharply. In that trough, the margins compressed and the business ran near-zero full-year profits. For a Q4 franchise (EUZ.DE, FAA.VI), the revenue floor barely moves; for JOUT, it can move 30-40%.

Also: gross margin 38.8% in a good quarter is solid but not franchise-grade (cf. FAA.VI 65%+ software margins, EKF 51% diagnostic consumables). Product gross margins in branded outdoor equipment are compressed by COGS (motors, electronics, injection-molded plastics) vs. software or reagents.

Garmin's entry into the trolling motor market (Garmin Force, now Generation 3 Kraken) is a genuine competitive development. Garmin integrates with Garmin's own chartplotter/fish finder ecosystem (superior map quality) and has been growing share in the premium segment. The extent of Minn Kota share loss (if any) from Garmin Force is the single most important unverified data point.

**Quality summary:** Solid, defensible multi-brand portfolio with genuine market leadership. Family control ensures long-term orientation. Score 3/5 — does not qualify for CORE (Q<4 required).

### F — Downside Floor: **4/5**

The fortress balance sheet is exceptional:
- $107.9M cash, zero debt as of Q2 FY2026 = net cash per share ~$10/sh
- At $45/share, net cash is ~22% of market cap
- Company paid dividends even through the FY2022-2025 trough ($1.32/yr maintained)
- Family control (50%+) eliminates dilution risk entirely
- Johnson family has never done a dilutive deal, serial acquisition, or ATM offering in 55+ years

At trough, the business earns because even in bad years (FY2025 H1 = -$13M loss), H2 recovers on fishing season volume. Full-year net income was positive even in FY2024-2025 downturns. The $107.9M cash alone puts a hard floor at ~$10/share independent of business value.

**Why not 5/5:** The floor is still consumer discretionary dependent. Unlike GENC (floor is $155M net cash on $218M cap = 71%) or EUZ.DE (NRC-mandated annual recalibration = recession-proof), JOUT's earnings can compress 80%+ in a severe consumer downturn. The "hard floor" is the cash + the durable brand premium, not a regulation-mandated recurring revenue stream.

### R — Re-Rate Likelihood & Proximity: **2/5**

**Near-term catalyst (early August 2026):** Q3 FY2026 results cover the peak fishing season (April-July 2026). With H1 FY2026 at +21.5% revenue growth and GM expanding 370-680bps YoY, Q3 should be strong. If Q3 delivers another 15-20% revenue beat on strong demand, that confirms the fishing market recovery narrative.

**BUT why R=2/5:**
1. The single analyst has a **Hold** with PT $45-55 — right at current price. Even a strong Q3 beat pushes the PT to maybe $60-65 = 35-45% upside, not 2x.
2. Stock has already recovered from $30.35 (52-wk low) to ~$45 = **+48% from trough**. Some of the recovery is already priced.
3. **2x from $45 requires ~$90 = $950M cap.** At ~$680M FY2026 revenue (H1 run-rate), that's 1.4x P/S — achievable only with sustained multi-quarter earnings recovery AND analyst initiation AND P/E expansion. FY2022 peak was ~$90-100/share on $5-6 EPS — getting there again is an 18-24+ month story.
4. No new analyst initiation imminent; no M&A approach signaled; no Minn Kota product platform inflection event analogous to GENC's $60M backlog record.

**Base rate:** Multi-brand outdoor sporting goods niche leaders re-rated 3 of 4 from post-boom trough (Brunswick 4x from 2009 trough over 5yr; Callaway 2x in 18mo from 2020 trough; American Outdoor Brands 1.5x from 2022 trough; Malibu Boats 2x from 2023 trough). Swing factor = **consumer credit environment + new product launch cycle triggering replacement demand vs. inventory normalization pace**. The base rate is positive but timing is measured in years, not quarters.

**Base rate:** "Multi-brand outdoor/sporting goods niche leaders re-rated 3 of 4 from post-consumer-boom trough; swing factor = consumer credit access + new product cycle trigger that resets replacement demand urgency. Average re-rate timeline: 18-30 months from trough, not 0-6 months."
