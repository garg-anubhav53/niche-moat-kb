## Business Description

CML Microsystems Plc (AIM: CML) is a **70-year-old British fabless semiconductor company** designing proprietary ICs for niche professional communications and GNSS equipment. It sells finished chips to OEMs — it is NOT a pure IP licensor (like ARM) but a chip-product company that also takes NRE contracts for custom designs.

**Product and end-market mix (estimated — company does not disclose segment revenue):**

| Market | Description | Est. Revenue Share | Trend |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| PMR / Critical Comms | TETRA, DMR, dPMR, NXDN processor chips for police/fire/utilities handheld radios | ~50–60% | Slowly declining; LTE/4G PTT displacement secular headwind |
| GNSS / Satellite | GPS correction data radios, VSAT, AIS marine; new 12-yr contract won July 2025 | ~25–30% | Growing; $30M GNSS contract provides 12-yr revenue visibility |
| mmWave / RF (MwT acquired Oct 2023) | GaAs/GaN amplifiers for 5G NR / FWA (26.5–29.5 GHz), Ka-band satellite | ~10–15% | Growing; IMS2026 and SATShow 2026 launches; design-win stage |
| Wireline telephony | Analog/digital POTS/PSTN-era telephone ICs (NOT DSL/vectoring — mischaracterisation) | ~5–10% | Secular decline |

**One reportable segment (semiconductors):** No voluntary revenue breakdown disclosed. Customer names not disclosed. GNSS customer is anonymous but on LSE/AIM regulatory record.

**Headquarters:** Oval Park, Langford, Essex, UK. MwT subsidiary in Fremont, CA (GaAs/GaN chip fab partnerships).

## Moat Assessment

### PMR Qualification Barrier — Real and Durable at 3–5yr

1. **Multi-standard single platform:** CML's "common platform processor" supports ALL major PMR standards (TETRA, DMR, dPMR, NXDN, ARIB) from a single chip design. CML is a founding member of the dPMR Association and active ETSI participant. No other chip vendor covers this multi-standard breadth at CML's production volumes and price point.

2. **OEM design-in switching cost:** PMR radio qualification (TÜV, ETSI, body-authority type approval) takes 12–24 months. Once CML's chip is designed into a Hytera, Kenwood, or Tait radio platform, switching to an alternative supplier requires a complete chipset redesign + full type-approval re-certification. Average replacement cycles: 3–5 years per socket. This is a genuine PROCESS moat.

3. **Niche-size protection:** TETRA/DMR chips serve low millions of units per year — far too small for Qualcomm, Broadcom, or MediaTek to pursue. CML occupies the niche by virtue of incumbency and scale that is right-sized for the market.

4. **Moat durability at 3–5yr:** MODERATE-HIGH. The PMR installed base is sticky; body-authority certifications persist; OEM contract cycles are long. Qualcomm/MediaTek will not enter this market.

5. **Moat durability at 10yr:** MODERATE. Long-term structural risk: LTE/4G PTT (push-to-talk over LTE via MCPTT, used by UK Emergency Services Network, FirstNet in the US) is gradually displacing narrowband PMR in new government contracts. This is a 15–20 year cycle, not imminent, but it is directional. Revenue has already stagnated: FY2024 flat, FY2025 flat, FY2026 -11%.

**Moat verdict: REAL at 3–5yr via qualification barrier and switching cost. Structurally challenged at 10yr as PMR market (50–60% of revenue) faces LTE displacement. MwT/GNSS diversification is the right strategic response but unproven in revenue contribution.**

## Financial Quality Assessment

**Revenue trend: DECLINING.** After flat FY2025 (£22.90m) vs FY2024 (£22.89m), revenue fell 10.7% to £20.45m in FY2026 (year ended March 2026). Shore Capital FY2027 forecast: £23.9m (+17%) — single-analyst optimism, not consensus.

**Gross margin: COMPRESSED.** FY2025: 69% (£15.89m GP). FY2026: 63% (£12.89m GP). The ~600bp compression is entirely explained by an unusually high NRE mix in FY2026 driven by the GNSS contract design phase. As NRE work completes and product volume ramps, gross margin should recover toward 65–70%. This is temporary — but "temporary" depends on the NRE phase completing successfully.

**Profitability: NEGATIVE.** FY2026 adjusted PBT: **-£1.76m (loss).** FY2026 statutory PBT +£1.80m — but this is entirely attributable to a £3.56m land disposal gain (£7m total land proceeds split over FY2025/FY2026). The "return to profit" headline is economically misleading. FY2025 adjusted PBT: +£0.88m (marginally profitable).

**Cash: STRONG.** £12.80m cash, zero debt, debt-free balance sheet. Net cash = 29% of the £44m market cap. The increase in cash from £9.92m (FY2025) to £12.80m (FY2026) despite an operating loss is entirely due to the £3.0m Tranche 2 land proceeds. Organic FCF in FY2026 is estimated negative (operating loss + capex) — the cash balance increase is non-recurring.

**Dividends: 11p/share (4.2% yield at 265p).** 5p interim + 6p final. FY2026 final dividend ex-div August 6, 2026. The dividend is covered by the cash balance (annual dividend ~£1.83m vs £12.80m cash) even if operations remain loss-making, but it is not covered by earnings.

**Analyst coverage: 1 broker only — Shore Capital Group (House Stock / NOMAD).** Target price: **223p** (reissued April 4, 2026) — **16% BELOW the current market price of 265p.** Shore Capital has had the opportunity to revise upward since April and chose not to. FY2027 Shore Capital estimates: revenue £23.9m, adj PBT ~-£0.1m (near breakeven), EPS ~0.2p.
