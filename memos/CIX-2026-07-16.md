## Company

CompX International (NYSE American: CIX) manufactures two product lines:
- **Security Products** (~60–65% of revenue): OEM-specified, customer-keyed security locks and access-control hardware embedded in industrial cabinets — telecom equipment enclosures, medical carts, gaming machines, utility metering, and HVAC controls.
- **Marine Components** (~35–40% of revenue): Stainless steel fittings, hardware, and propulsion-control components for recreational and industrial marine applications (pontoon boats, deck boats, commercial vessels).

Majority owner: **Contran Corporation (~85%)**, itself controlled by the Harold Simmons estate/NL Industries structure. Near-zero public float. No institutional interest. Zero sell-side coverage.

## The 5 Asymmetry Questions

### 1. Is the moat real and durable?

**Security Products: YES — spec'd-in OEM lock.** CompX designs its keys and cylinders to customer-specific key codes and security levels. Once a customer designs a CompX lock into their OEM product platform (cabinet model, cart model), switching requires: (a) sourcing an alternative with identical keyway, (b) re-engineering cabinet tolerances, (c) re-qualifying the substitute with their end customer, and (d) managing keying inventory transition. Minimum 12–18 months and $500K–$2M NRE for large OEM programs. This is a genuine design-lock moat.

**Marine Components: WEAKER.** Recreational marine hardware (stainless fittings, propulsion controls) has moderate switching cost (boat OEM certifies components) but lower specificity than lock keyways. "Industrial market" growth in Q1 2026 is encouraging but mechanism unclear. Marine moat is PROCESS + moderate SOLE_SOURCE for some programs, not textbook.

**Who's tried and failed?** Major lock manufacturers (Kaba, Allegion, Abloy) serve commercial/institutional markets with different security levels; they rarely compete for the OEM cabinet niche that CIX dominates (1–5M unit runs, specific key codes, low per-unit pricing). No known sustained competitive attack on CIX's OEM lock base.

**Moat verdict:** Real and durable on Security Products (70%+ of moat value). Marine moat is secondary.

### 2. What is the floor?

**Strong earnings floor.**
- Q1 2026 net income $5.9M on $40.6M revenue = **14.5% net margin** (improving)
- Annualized: ~$23.6M net income
- Balance sheet: Contran structure typically means lean operations; no publicly disclosed debt on CIX balance sheet; cash positive
- No dilution risk: Contran ~85% → zero incentive to dilute; share count stable
- Dividend: CIX historically pays dividends; confirms profitability at current operating level
- Floor at "zero new orders": Security Products OEM business is sticky (customers don't redesign cabinets every year). Even in a severe industrial capex recession, CIX would earn on replacement/aftermarket needs.

**Floor verdict: CONFIRMED.** Earnings floor ~$18–20M net income at trough (assuming revenue decline to ~$155M; 12% net margin).

### 3. What is the specific 2x re-rating catalyst and timing?

**THIS IS THE CRITICAL FAILURE.** No hard-dated <12-month catalyst exists.

Possible paths to 2x (all speculative, no timeline):
- **Contran going-private:** Take CIX private at 1.5–2x current P/E (~$430–570M). No signal. Has not happened despite years of 12x PE trading.
- **Analyst initiation:** With 0 public float, structurally impossible to attract institutional coverage. No change expected.
- **Revenue acceleration:** Marine industrial market growth could drive 10–15% revenue growth, warranting P/E re-rating to 15–18x. But Q1 was +0.7% revenue — acceleration not confirmed.
- **Sector re-rating:** If industrial infrastructure spending boom creates visible demand surge, CIX could be re-rated with the group. But CIX's PE is already at sector lows — no identifiable trigger.

**2x catalyst verdict: ABSENT.** The Contran 85% ownership creates a structural valuation discount that can persist indefinitely. Price was ~$23/share in July 2026 (est. from cap $286M ÷ 12.3M shares). The stock has likely traded at 10–14x PE for years with no catalyst to compress that discount.

### 4. What must be true for the bull case, and is it already priced?

**Bull case requires:**
1. Contran reduces ownership or executes a going-private at a control premium → this re-rates CIX to 16–20x PE ($380–472M cap, 33–65% upside from $286M) — plausible ceiling for a 2x
2. New large industrial/data-center infrastructure program (AI racks, grid equipment, EV charging stations) spec's CIX locks into tens of millions of new enclosures → revenue +30% sustained
3. Marine Components wins defined large OEM contract for commercial marine/industrial → visible in guidance

**Is it priced?** At 12x PE, the discount is already priced in. The stock is cheap vs. the business quality, but cheapness alone is not a 2x catalyst. The P/E has probably been 10–14x for many years; there's no sign the market is about to realize the "mispricing."

---

### 5. What kills the thesis?

**Primary kill:** Contran never reduces ownership → stock stays cheap indefinitely. No forced re-rating. This alone is sufficient to make CIX a value trap at any time horizon under 5 years.

**Secondary kills:**
- Major OEM customer (gaming machine maker, telecom OEM) redesigns product platform and qualifies a competitor's lock
- Marine recreational market weakens 20%+ (housing + consumer discretionary downturn) → Marine segment profitability evaporates
- Rising steel/aluminum costs (tariff exposure) compress Security Products margins
