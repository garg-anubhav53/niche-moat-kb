---

## Q1: Is the moat real and durable?

**CONFIRMED — but not textbook sole-source.**

KVH's FOG business has a genuine PROCESS moat:
- One of ~4–5 commercial-scale FOG manufacturers globally (KVH, iXblue/Exail FR, Honeywell, Silicon Sensing/BAE Systems)
- Proprietary fiber winding process + coil geometry → precision compact gyros at commercial volumes none of the large-defense players bother to target cost-effectively
- 25,000+ units → calibration dataset and algorithm advantage; platform designers spec in KVH FOGs and requalification takes 6–18 months in autonomous/drone programs
- Growing commercial markets: autonomous vehicles, UAS/drones, survey vessels, agricultural robots, industrial stabilization
- +70% FOG sales growth × 2 consecutive quarters demonstrates genuine demand, not one order

**Who's tried and failed:** MEMS gyroscope makers (Analog Devices, STMicro, Bosch) compete on cost but cannot match FOG precision for demanding inertial navigation. Chinese FOG producers exist but face qualification/trust barriers in US/European commercial programs.

**Key uncertainty:** "Tactical navigation" revenue mix post-VSAT. If defense is >50% of post-divestiture revenue, this becomes a SECTOR concern (defense-primary). KVH's heritage and stated strategy emphasize commercial markets, but the composition of "tactical" revenue is unverified.

## Q2: What is the floor?

**CONDITIONAL — improving but not a fortress.**

- 2026 EBITDA guidance: $11–16M (~8–11% margin at $140M midpoint revenue)
- Q1 2026 EPS: $0.02 → barely net-income positive
- Positive equity; no going-concern signals; no major debt flags
- Post-VSAT divestiture: less recurring subscription revenue; floor is now hardware + services from installed base

**Scenario at zero new orders:** Maritime connectivity hardware generates some service revenue from installed vessel operators, but FOG is project-driven. Estimated trough EBITDA $5–8M → at $193M cap = ~24–38x EV/EBITDA on trough. Not a cheap floor; the stock needs continued FOG growth to justify valuation.

**Assessment:** Earns on current volumes but NOT on zero new orders in a meaningful way. Net income floor ≈ near-zero.

## Q3: Specific 2x Re-Rating Catalyst + Timing?

**PROXIMATE BUT VAGUE** — no single hard-dated 2x event.

1. **Q2 2026 earnings (~Jul/Aug 2026):** If FOG growth sustains +50%+ for 3rd consecutive quarter AND EBITDA at high end of guidance AND net income solidly positive → analyst initiations possible → stock re-rates
2. **Narrative reframe:** From "marine VSAT survivor" to "pure FOG/inertial navigation platform" deserving 15–25x EV/EBITDA vs. current ~12–17x → multiple expansion without revenue acceleration
3. **Acquisition:** Post-divestiture clean FOG company → attractive to Teledyne, L3Harris, Thales for commercial nav exposure; strategic premium probable

**Why NOT 2x in <6 months:**

- No regulatory cert, no hard-dated contract, no analyst initiation already announced
- Beat-and-raise on Q2 likely reaches $250–280M cap (30–45% upside), not 2x

## Q4: What must be true? Already priced?

**Not priced in** — valuation is reasonable, not stretched.

At $193M cap on $130–145M guided revenue = 1.3–1.5x P/S. For a company growing FOG 70%+ with improving EBITDA, this is not expensive. The market is pricing in recovery, not hypergrowth multiple. The 2x requires: (1) continued FOG growth acceleration × 4+ quarters, (2) P/S expansion to 2.5–3x, (3) net income growing from $0.02 EPS to $0.40+ EPS — achievable in 12–18 months but NOT guaranteed in a single <6-month catalyst.

---

## Q5: What kills it?

1. **Defense concentration undisclosed:** If post-VSAT "tactical navigation" is predominantly DoD → SECTOR_KILL; we are investing in a defense company under a commercial label
2. **FOG growth deceleration:** Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 were +70%; Q2 2026 at +25% or lower breaks the sustained-inflection narrative
3. **Revenue gap from VSAT divestiture:** If services contributed profitable EBITDA that's not being replaced by FOG hardware volume, guidance $130–145M proves optimistic
4. **MEMS improvement curve:** Continued MEMS precision improvement at lower cost could erode the 5–10-year moat window for lower-tier FOG applications
