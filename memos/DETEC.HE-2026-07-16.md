# DETEC.HE — Detection Technology Oyj — §5 Deep-Dive
**Date:** 2026-07-16 · **Scout score:** 7/12 · **Final Grade:** B · **Tier:** CANDIDATE
**Sector:** Industrial Precision (X-ray Detector Components OEM) · **Exchange:** Nasdaq Helsinki

---

## Situation in One Paragraph

DETEC is a ~€132M-cap Finnish X-ray detector-array OEM (silicon + CdTe multi-energy/photon-counting) selling design-in components to CT OEMs across **Medical**, **Security**, and **Industrial** end-markets. FY2025 was a **cyclical down-year** — revenue €101M (−6%), net income €6.4M (−42%), net margin 6.4% (from 10% in FY2024) — driven almost entirely by a collapse in the **Security** segment (its largest, ~40%+ of sales; down ~28% in the weak quarters on soft China smart-security/cargo demand and FX headwinds). **Q1 2026 inflected hard**: revenue €25.3M (+13.5% reported / ~+8.6% organic), net income €1.94M (+133%), margin back to 7.7%, all regions positive with EMEIA at a record. Management guides YoY growth in Q2 and Q3 2026. The balance sheet is pristine (net cash, current ratio 4.8, ROI 14.7%, minimal debt), and a €0.30 dividend (68% payout, ~4.5% yield, 10-year unbroken streak) is proposed. **The re-rate question is binary and dated: does the H1 2026 report on 6 August 2026 confirm the cyclical recovery is real and structural, or was Q1 a one-quarter FX/restocking bounce?**

---

## 1. Sub-Scores (Q / F / R / C)

### Q — Business Quality: **3/5** (solid, defensible — but cyclical and pricing-constrained)

**Evidence:**
- **Returns/margins**: ROI 14.7%; net margins swing 6–10% across the cycle; EBITA compressed to ~6.7% in weak H1 2025 and recovered to ~12% EBITDA by Q4 2025. Respectable but not franchise-grade returns.
- **Moat durability**: The PROCESS moat is real but *narrow*. Design-in/requalification lock (12–24mo) creates genuine switching friction and a 30-year OEM track record supports it — but DETEC is a mid-size component supplier squeezed between much larger customers (GE HealthCare, Siemens Healthineers, Canon Medical, security integrators) and larger detector rivals (Varex Imaging, Hamamatsu, ams OSRAM). Management explicitly flags **"price competition especially in China"** — pricing power is limited, and some FY2024 margin was cyclical rent, not durable pricing power.
- **Reinvestment runway**: Positive — photon-counting/multi-energy CT is a secular growth vector (~10.5% CAGR, ~$500M by 2033); Medical grew ~14% in Q1 2026 and Industrial (TFT panels, industrial inspection) is expanding. But top-line has been **roughly flat-to-down over 3 years** (€108M→€101M) — this is a cyclical mid-cap, not a compounder.
- **Capital allocation**: Disciplined — net cash, consistent well-covered dividend, no empire-building. Good but unspectacular.

**Score rationale:** Moat mechanism is real (PROCESS + OEM design-in barrier); quality of the business is solid with durable margins and a clean balance sheet; but cyclicality, pricing pressure from Chinese competitors, and revenue stagnation over 3 years prevent a 4 or 5. This is a good business — not a franchise.

---

### F — Downside Floor: **4/5** (earns through trough on net cash — hard to lose money permanently)

**Evidence:**
- **Earnings floor**: Stayed **solidly profitable in the worst of the down-cycle** (€6.4M net income in a year where net income fell 42%; positive EBIT every quarter). This is an **earnings floor**, not merely an asset floor.
- **Balance sheet**: Net cash, current ratio 4.8, minimal interest-bearing debt. **No going-concern, delisting, or dilution risk whatsoever.**
- **Dividend**: €0.30/share for FY2025 (covered at 68% payout, ~4.5% yield at €8.70-9.04 current price), 10-year unbroken streak — this sets a hard downside cushion.
- **One notch off 5**: Cyclicality is real — margins can halve in a downturn, and the Security/China swing can deepen. Trough *earnings power* is soft even though *solvency* is rock-solid. A deeper cyclical downturn than FY2025 could push net income below €4M.

### R — Re-Rate Likelihood: **4/5** (dated near-term catalyst + moderate valuation gap)

**Evidence:**
- **Catalyst with a hard date**: **H1 2026 report scheduled for 6 August 2026 (~3 weeks).** Q1 already printed +133% net income; management guided Q2/Q3 growth. A confirming H1 print directly closes the "is the recovery structural" question and forces consensus estimate revisions upward.
- **Valuation gap**: Consensus analyst average target ~€12.18 (range €10.5–14.7; all three Helsinki analysts at Buy). Current price ~€8.70-9.04 implies **~35–40% gap to consensus average** and potentially 60%+ to the high target. Trades at ~12x normalized (FY2024) earnings and <1x EV/sales given net cash, plus a 4.5% yield.
- **Discovery runway**: Small but not zero — 3 Helsinki analysts, English-language quarterly releases. A clean H1 beat can move it materially; a tariff/FX-muted print will cap it.
- **NOT a 5**: The ~35-40% gap to consensus is NOT a 2x. The upside to normalized valuation is real but moderate — the Security/China recovery and FX headwinds could blunt the catalyst. A 2x would require multiples to expand well beyond current analyst targets plus earnings recovery, requiring several quarters of compounding.

### C — Confidence: **4/5** (high-quality, verifiable disclosure; a few open unknowns)

**Evidence:**

  2. **Tellurium/CdTe supply resilience + quantified US-tariff impact** — flagged qualitatively by management, not sized in EUR terms.
  3. **Durability of the Q1 2026 rebound** — is the Security segment recovery structural (China demand returned) or a one-quarter FX/restocking bounce? Only H1 (6 Aug) can confirm.

## 5. Final Verdict

**GRADE: B** — High on three of four sub-scores (F4/R4/C4) with one soft spot (Q3: cyclical, pricing-constrained OEM component supplier, not a franchise-quality business). Falls short of Grade A because A requires Q≥4 AND F≥4; Q=3 blocks it.

**TIER: CANDIDATE** — Per ROUTINE.md WATCH/CATALYST bar, the upside is ~35% to consensus average (€12.18) — this does NOT meet the "legitimate chance to ~2x QUICKLY" requirement. The 2x would require analyst targets at €17+ to be right AND the stock reaching them within the catalyst window. Analyst targets are €10.5–14.7. Per ROUTINE.md: "if the 2x needs the whole industry cycle to turn, it is CANDIDATE at best." That is exactly this situation. Method.md grades TIER: CATALYST (R≥4, dated near-term re-rate, F≥3), but ROUTINE.md correctly places this as **CANDIDATE** given the 35% not-2x upside profile.

**What makes it interesting:** A net-cash, dividend-cushioned (4.5% yield) process-moat OEM at ~12x normalized earnings with a hard 6-Aug catalyst that could materially narrow the 35-40% gap to consensus — and with a buy zone that currently exists. The floor is real (earns at trough, net cash). The quality is adequate (Q=3, not a value trap). The entry today at ~€8.70-9.04 is reasonably asymmetric even if short of a 2x setup.

**Asymmetry-to-risk in one sentence:** A net-cash, 4.5%-yielding PROCESS-moat OEM at ~12x normalized earnings with a hard 6-Aug-2026 catalyst to confirm cyclical recovery and ~35% gap to consensus average — favorable asymmetry with a hard downside floor, but upside capped by weak pricing power and China/tellurium cyclicality; CANDIDATE, not a quick-2x WATCH.
