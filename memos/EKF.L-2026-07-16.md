### Five Asymmetry Questions

**1. Is the moat real and durable?**

YES — two confirmed mechanisms, neither textbook monopoly but both genuinely sticky:

- **POC hematology consumable lock-in**: DiaSpect and HemoControl hemoglobin analyzers are designed to accept only EKF-validated reagent cuvettes/strips. Any clinical site switching to a competitor (HemoCue/Danaher) must re-validate the new device under CE-IVD + local lab accreditation requirements — typically 3-6 months of parallel testing. NHS and African/Asian ministry procurement cycles reinforce stickiness. HemoCue is the main competitor (stronger in Nordic hospitals); EKF wins on price-sensitive markets (Africa, NHS primary care, anemia screening programs). The lock-in is real but not absolute.

- **β-HB enzyme CDMO (stronger moat)**: EKF's Stanbio subsidiary manufactures specialty β-hydroxybutyrate (β-HB) enzymes sold to diagnostic companies who incorporate them into ketone assay formulations. Once a diagnostic customer's product is validated with EKF's specific enzyme lot/specification, switching to another supplier requires full regulatory re-validation of the customer's diagnostic product — a costly, 12-24 month process. FY2025 results confirmed a *new fermentation development agreement with a major diagnostics customer* expected to contribute further in 2026. This CDMO angle is a genuine high-switching-cost relationship.

Who has tried and failed? HemoCue (now Radiometer/Danaher) competes directly on POC hemoglobin — they are well-resourced and have tried to take NHS contracts but EKF has retained volume through price competitiveness. No public evidence of a β-HB enzyme competitor displacing EKF from a customer formulation.

**Moat score: 1/2** — real, two-mechanism, but HemoCue competes and the CDMO scale is still modest.

**5. What kills it?**

- **NHS de-listing of POC hemoglobin testing** — NHS has historically been skeptical of excessive POC testing and has rationalized away some products. A commissioning body decision to require central lab hemoglobin testing only would cut EKF's NHS revenues significantly.
- **HemoCue price competition** — Danaher has deep pockets. If they discount aggressively below EKF's cost structure in price-sensitive markets, EKF loses volume without a margin buffer large enough to sustain.
- **Fermentation CDMO agreement is one-off** — The "major diagnostics customer" agreement is not named and terms are undisclosed. If this is a one-year development contract with no follow-on production, CDMO revenue disappears post-2026.
- **AIM illiquidity + UK small-cap discount** — UK AIM small-caps trade at structural discounts (low liquidity, no index inclusion, 1 analyst). Re-rating requires either analyst coverage expansion or M&A interest — both uncertain.
