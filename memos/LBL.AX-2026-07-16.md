## Business in one paragraph
LaserBond Ltd (ASX: LBL) is an Australian specialty surface-engineering business with 30+ years building process IP in laser cladding and thermal-spray hard-facing of industrial wear parts (mining, oil & gas, transport, agriculture). The business has four segments: **Services** (reclamation/repair of worn components at LaserBond facilities), **Products** (manufactured parts incorporating laser cladding), **Technology** (sale and licensing of LaserBond cladding systems + process know-how to OEM customers), and **R&D**. H1 FY2026 (6 months to Dec 2025): revenue A$23M (+13.4% YoY), 54% gross margin (+600bps YoY), EBITDA A$5.2M (+58%), NPAT A$2.2M (+117%). The Technology segment clinched a **A$2.4M Komatsu licensing deal** (delivery/commissioning + invoice in H2 FY2026) — the first major confirmation that LaserBond's process IP can be monetised independent of direct service revenue. LaserBond also holds a minority stake in Gateway Industrial Products (equity-accounted profit A$373K in H1), whose own laser cell is improving the margin mix over time. Cap ~A$64M (~US$42M); 2 ASX analysts (both Strong Buy, A$0.85 PT vs A$0.54 price = 57% upside to target). FY2026 annual results not yet released (FY ends June 30, 2026; July 16 is only 2.5 weeks post year-end).

## The Moat: PROCESS (laser cladding IP + OEM licensing)

Laser cladding is a precision surface-engineering process depositing hard alloys (WC-Co, NiCrBSi, chromium carbide) onto worn industrial parts using a high-power laser. LaserBond's moat is **PROCESS not SOLE_SOURCE**: the core competitive advantage is 30 years of proprietary process parameters, alloy formulation knowledge, metallurgical know-how, and customer qualification — not a regulatory certification or monopoly product. Multiple global competitors exist (Kennametal, Hoganas, Castolin Eutectic, Praxair Surface Technologies) but few operate at LaserBond's process maturity across Australia's industrial heartland, and none have demonstrated a comparable licensing model with Tier-1 OEM buy-in (Komatsu). The Komatsu deal is the key moat validator: a global mining OEM paying A$2.4M to license LaserBond's process technology and install a LaserBond laser cell confirms that (a) the process IP is proprietary, (b) it cannot be easily replicated internally at comparable quality/cost, and (c) LaserBond can extract recurring licensing revenue from installed cells. Risk: at-scale OEM customers could eventually develop competing capability (Kromek/Siemens-type in-source risk). Near-term R&D priorities include tungsten carbide supply-chain alternatives (China-dominated WC supply) and new markets (wind turbines, agriculture).
