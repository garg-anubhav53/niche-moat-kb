## Five Asymmetry Questions

### 1. Is the moat real and durable?
**YES — confirmed SOLE_SOURCE OEM handpiece design-in.**

Pro-Dex's powered surgical handpieces are custom-engineered for specific OEM robotic/surgical systems (orthopedic, thoracic). Once designed in:
- Re-engineering the mating interface requires a redesign cycle of 6–12 months
- Re-validation requires biocompatibility + performance testing
- New FDA submission (PMA supplement or 510(k) amendment citing the new handpiece) required — timeline 12–24 months
- Switching cost to OEM: $500K–$2M+ in engineering and regulatory time

Evidence of durability: the largest customer has maintained a multi-year relationship across product generations. FY2025 +24% revenue. Q1 FY2026: $1.40 EPS vs $0.50 estimate (180% beat). Q3 FY2026: $1.20 EPS vs $0.62 estimate (93.55% beat). The customer is not cutting — they are ramping.

Who's tried and failed? The autoclave-compatible precision torque handpiece niche is served by Stryker, Conmed in-house, and a handful of custom OEM shops. PDEX's advantage is 35+ years of specialization in this exact product category and FDA device history embedded in customer submissions.

**Moat rating: 1/2** — real, mechanism clear; BUT concentrated in one primary OEM customer (limited diversification reduces moat durability score).

### 2. What is the floor?
**Conditional positive earnings — not fortress.**

- Q3 FY2026: Revenue $19.9M (+15% YoY), GM 20% (declined from 27% in FY2025 due to product mix + tariff headwinds)
- TTM EPS: $3.63 → TTM net income ~$11.6M (for ~3.19M shares)
- Profitability: positive at current volumes; Q1 FY2026 EPS $1.40; Q3 FY2026 EPS $1.20
- Backlog as of Q3 end (March 2026): ~$46.8M
- Monogram Orthopedics: PDEX holds equity in pre-commercial robotic surgery startup; valuation gains distort EPS in favorable quarters

Floor concern: At 20% GM with single-customer concentration, a 30% revenue drop (largest customer curtailment) would:
- Revenue: $14M/quarter → $56M/year
- Gross profit at 20%: $11.2M/year
- OpEx: ~$7–8M/year estimated
- Operating income: ~$3–4M → thin positive
- Net income including investment income: positive but small

This is a CONDITIONAL positive floor, not a fortress. The 20% GM is far below the 40%+ threshold preferred; a sustained 20% GM would degrade quality score to 0. However, the pattern of Q1/Q3 EPS beats at $1.20–$1.40/quarter implies the realized net income is stronger than GM alone suggests (investment income, tax benefits, or product mix that's better in some quarters).

**Floor rating: 1/2** — positive equity, profitable at current volumes, no leverage; conditional on largest customer continuity and on GM not staying at 20%.

But the analyst community is at $70 1-yr target (17% upside), suggesting they don't see the same EPS trajectory. The asymmetry exists IF Q4 beats, which has not yet occurred.
