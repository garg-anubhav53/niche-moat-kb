## Company Profile

Castings plc (AIM: CGS.L) is a UK-listed specialist iron foundry manufacturing spheroidal graphite (SG) iron and compacted graphite iron (CGI) castings primarily for commercial vehicle (CV) engine and powertrain OEMs. Core products: engine blocks, cylinder heads, structural powertrain housings, and large iron castings for CV manufacturers (DAF, Mercedes-Benz Trucks, Volvo Trucks, IVECO). Operations: Brownhills (West Midlands) and Chesterfield foundries. AIM-listed; no US listing.

**FY2026 (year ended April 2026) headline results (AIM RNS, June 2026):**
- Revenue: ~£130M (–2% YoY on commercial vehicle production headwinds)
- Pre-tax profit (PBT): £10.3M (+84% YoY; doubled from FY2025 £5.6M)
- EPS: 17.36p
- Cash from operations: £25.1M (strong OCF despite revenue contraction)
- Net debt: zero; dividend maintained throughout CV downturn
- Capital allocation note: acquired Chamberlin plc foundry assets in administration for £400K (competitor eliminated; equipment/patterns acquired)

The profit surge on declining revenue reflects operating leverage (energy cost normalisation, input cost reduction, cost control), plus incidental margin improvement from the Chamberlin distressed-asset purchase. CV electrification is the structural headwind.

**Share price at time of analysis:** ~330p  
**Analyst consensus PT:** 341.7p (9 analyst estimates; +3.5% upside — stock effectively at fair value)  
**Market cap:** ~£85M (~$110M USD); AIM

## Historical Base Rate / Analog Process

Comparable situations: UK SG/CGI iron foundry with OEM CV engine specification + EV transition headwind:

1. **Chamberlin plc (AIM: CMH)** — UK iron casting foundry supplying CV engine and industrial components. Outcome: administration 2022, ceased operations. Swing factor: leverage + EV transition + energy cost spike = terminal.
2. **Nordic Iron Casting / European Tier-2 foundries** — European CGI/SG foundries supplying Volvo/Scania CV programs; operated through 2022-24 volume trough but no public market re-rating; most privately held or acquired by PE.
3. **SinterCast AB (SINT.ST, in KB)** — CGI process control licensor; base rate 1/4 re-rated in public markets; same EV headwind on ICE engine blocks; swing = new CGI application or strategic acquirer.
4. **Fonderia di Torbole / Meehanite Group** — Continental iron foundries serving CV programs; range-bound in secondary markets; acquired at modest multiples (~8-12x PE) by industrial PE.

**Base rate:** "CV OEM supplier foundries re-rated 1/4 in 2015-2025; 3/4 either remained range-bound or were bankrupted by the cycle. Swing factor = customer diversification away from pure CV/diesel AND electrification timeline longer than market expects."

## Sub-Assessment Scores

**Q — Business Quality: 3/5**
Solid, defensible foundry business: PBT +84% demonstrates real operating leverage; £25.1M OCF strong. BUT 6% PBT margin on £130M revenue is modest — not a franchise compounder. Revenue –2% YoY on structural CV headwind. SG/CGI iron casting is specialty (not commodity pressed steel) with genuine switching costs. Wind energy and other industrial castings appear to be a growing non-CV segment but undisclosed size. Chamberlin acquisition signals disciplined capital allocation at cycle trough.

**F — Downside Floor: 4/5**
Earnings floor confirmed and genuine: £10.3M PBT in a difficult down-revenue year; £25.1M OCF; no bank debt; dividend maintained. Floor does not rely on asset/book value alone — the company is actively profitable in a headwind year. Minus 1 from perfect: CV electrification is a structural 5-7yr headwind that could compress earnings floor if truck engine block volumes decline materially.

**R — Re-Rate Likelihood: 1/5**
Stock at 330p vs analyst consensus PT 341.7p = 3.5% upside only. No asymmetric entry. No hard-dated catalyst inside 6 months. Next results (H1 FY2027) are >12 months away. 9 analyst estimates indicates meaningful coverage — not the 1-2 originally estimated, reducing the coverage void thesis. CV EV headwind is structural and multi-year. 2x from 330p would require ~660p = ~£170M cap = ~38x FY2026 EPS = not supported by fundamentals at current trajectory.

**C — Confidence / Data Quality: 3/5**
Full AIM RNS results; UK GAAP audited; adequate financial disclosure for a small AIM company; June 2026 data = recent. 9 analyst estimates (more than the 1-2 originally assumed) → some institutional coverage. UNKNOWN: customer concentration (OEM split by revenue); UNKNOWN: FY2027 CV OEM production schedules; UNKNOWN: wind/industrial non-CV revenue as % of total. These are material gaps. English-language coverage exists but is AIM-specific only.
