# SEDANA.ST — Sedana Medical AB — Deep-Dive Memo
**Date:** 2026-07-20 · **Run:** #48 · **Analyst:** OPUS adversarial red-team · **Score:** 9/12 (Q2/F2/R3/C2) · **Grade:** CANDIDATE C

---

## Thesis Summary
Sedana Medical is a Swedish single-product ICU med-tech/specialty-pharma play whose AnaConDa/Sedaconda ACD device delivers volatile sedation (isoflurane/sevoflurane) in the ICU without an anaesthesia machine, generating ~71% gross margin on a device + recurring consumable/drug razor-blade model with a dominant European installed base. The core asymmetry is a **discrete FDA NDA decision (Q1–Q2 2027)** into a US market management sizes at ~3x Europe, against a stock at 48% of its 52-week high after timeline slippage and cash-burn fears. The bear counterweight is that after 10+ years of EU commercialization the company still loses money and must raise dilutive capital before it can monetize the US catalyst.

---

## Moat Analysis
The moat is **real but narrow-and-shallow**: genuine on breadth (2.3M cumulative devices, ~84% share of actual users vs. ~14% MIRUS in French real-world data, NICE endorsement, 10+ years of clinical evidence, non-inferiority to propofol demonstrated in the SED001 Sedaconda pivotal), but shallow on economics — the franchise has not thrown off profit despite a decade in market. That persistent lossmaking is the single most important moat tell: either pricing power is weaker than the 71% GM implies (opex/clinical/commercial spend consumes it), or the addressable market is structurally small.

MIRUS by TIM GmbH is technically superior in dosing automation, monitoring, and desflurane support, but real-world adoption data shows MIRUS is **marginal, not a share-gainer** (84% Sedaconda vs 14% MIRUS, French VOL'ICU real-world survey). The "MIRUS is eating AnaConDa" narrative is **overstated** — MIRUS is a persistent niche irritant, not a moat-eroder. The provisional bear framing materially over-stated the MIRUS threat.

**On the NDA**: This is a **drug NDA** for Sedaconda (isoflurane as a pharmaceutical drug administered via the AnaConDa/ACD device), not a device PMA. Isoflurane is off-patent, but the combination drug-device data package and the multi-year clinical dossier are regulatory barriers that a competitor would need to replicate from scratch. The INSPiRE-ICU Phase III program tested broad adult ICU sedation (non-inferiority to propofol) — the "3x Europe TAM" case rests on a broad label, which is supported by the pivotal design. The "difficult-to-sedate" Early Access wording is FDA rescue-use pathway language, not necessarily the final label scope.

## Financial Assessment
FY2025: Revenue MSEK 200 (+12% organic), ~71% GM (gross profit ~MSEK 142), EBIT –32 MSEK → loss is opex/investment-driven (~MSEK 174 opex: US Phase III, NDA, commercial buildout), not broken EU unit economics. Cash MSEK 91 (end-2025) → ~MSEK 52 (Q2 2026) at ~MSEK 19/qtr burn = ~10–12 months runway (to ~Q2–Q3 2027), colliding with the FDA decision window.

**Cash floor analysis:** Cash is only ~SEK 0.52/share — nearly all of the SEK 8.74 price is franchise value, not protected by a cash floor. Unlike companies with net-cash > 20% of market cap, here the downside is essentially unprotected franchise-value-at-risk.

**Dilution math:** A MSEK 300–500 raise (typical to fund US launch to cash-flow inflection) at, say, SEK 7–8 implies ~40–70M new shares — **~40–70% dilution** on a 99.3M base. With a history of directed issues at a discount, existing minorities can be significantly diluted without pro-rata participation. This is the gating risk, not the FDA date.

## Risk-Adjusted Thesis
The path to 2x is mechanical and dated: Sedana completes a survivable capital raise, FDA approves the isoflurane/Sedaconda NDA at a broad ICU-sedation label in Q1–Q2 2027, and a 2027–2028 US launch begins converting a ~3x-Europe TAM into recurring drug-plus-consumable revenue, re-rating the stock toward the SEK 18 consensus and beyond on execution. What breaks it: a CRL or a label narrowed to "difficult-to-sedate" rescue use (which collapses the TAM), or a raise forced at distressed/directed-issue prices that dilutes 40–70% before the catalyst pays off. Because cash is only ~SEK 0.52/share, the downside is franchise-value-at-risk, not asset-protected — this is a probability-weighted asymmetric bet, not a floored one, and should be sized accordingly.

---

## Key Monitoring Triggers
1. **Capital raise terms** — announcement, size, structure (rights issue vs. directed), and clearing price. A pro-rata rights issue near market = de-risking; a discounted directed issue = confirmed RED FLAG and a lower re-entry.
2. **FDA action + label scope** — approval vs. CRL, and critically whether the granted indication is broad adult ICU sedation (INSPiRE-ICU basis) or narrowed to difficult-to-sedate/rescue use. Label breadth, not just approval, sets the TAM.
3. **Burn trajectory + EU organic growth** — quarterly cash burn and whether EU organic reaccelerates above ~12% or continues decelerating (EU saturation signal); an EU that self-funds more of the US build shrinks the required raise.

## Red Flags (2 of 12 modes)

**RED FLAG 1 — Imminent, potentially large and minority-unfriendly dilution.** ~10-month runway collides with the catalyst timeline. Cash ~SEK 0.52/share provides no floor. Company has documented history of directed share issues (615 MSEK + 375 MSEK) that bypass minority pre-emption rights. A distressed or directed raise could cost existing holders 40–70% dilution.

**RED FLAG 2 — 100% single-product concentration with no earnings floor.** One device franchise + its consumable/drug is the entire company. A CRL or narrow label leaves essentially no downside protection (no cash floor, no earnings floor). Positive skew is expectation-weighted, not structurally protected.
